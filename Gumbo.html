New Orleans Creole Gumbo

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup bacon drippings
  • 1 cup coarsely chopped celery
  • 1 large onion, coarsely chopped
  • 1 large green bell pepper, coarsely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lb andouille sausage, sliced
  • 3 quarts water
  • 6 cubes beef bouillon
  • 1 tbsp white sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp hot pepper sauce (e.g. Tabasco), or to taste
  • ½ tsp Cajun seasoning blend (e.g. Tony Chachere's), or to taste
  • 4 bay leaves
  • ½ tsp dried thyme leaves
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can stewed tomatoes
  • 1 (6 oz) can tomato sauce
  • 4 tsp file powder, divided
  • 2 tbsp bacon drippings
  • 2 (10 oz) packages frozen cut okra, thawed
  • 2 tbsp distilled white vinegar
  • 1 lb lump crabmeat
  • 3 lbs uncooked medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

Instructions

  1. Make the roux, whisk together flour and 3/4 cup bacon drippings in a large, heavy saucepan over medium-low heat until smooth
  2. Cook roux, whisking constantly, until it turns a rich mahogany brown color
  3. This can take 20-30 minutes; watch heat carefully and whisk constantly or roux will burn
  4. Remove from heat; continue whisking until mixture stops cooking
  5. Make the gumbo, place celery, onion, green bell pepper, and garlic into the work bowl of a food processor
  6. Pulse until all vegetables are very finely chopped
  7. Stir vegetables into roux, and mix in sliced sausage
  8. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring constantly, until vegetables are tender (~10-15 minutes)
  9. Remove from heat and set aside
  10. Combine water and beef bouillon cubes in a large Dutch oven or soup pot
  11. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat
  12. Stir until bouillon cubes dissolve, then whisk roux mixture into the boiling water
  13. Reduce heat to a simmer and mix in sugar, salt, hot pepper sauce, Cajun seasoning, bay leaves, thyme, stewed tomatoes, and tomato sauce
  14. Simmer soup over low heat for 1 hour
  15. Mix in 2 tsp of file gumbo powder at 45-minute mark
  16. Meanwhile, melt 2 tbsp bacon drippings in a skillet over medium heat
  17. Add okra and vinegar and cook for 15 minutes
  18. Remove okra with a slotted spoon and stir into the simmering gumbo
  19. Mix in crabmeat, shrimp, and Worcestershire sauce, and simmer until flavors have blended (45 more minutes)
  20. Stir in 2 more tsp of file gumbo powder just before serving
