Beef Bourguignon

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 3 cups Burgundy wine
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 tbsp brandy
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed
  • 10 whole black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 sprig fresh parsley
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 lbs cubed beef chuck roast

Bourguignon

  • 4 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • ¼ lb bacon, cubed
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 (10.5 oz) can beef broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 1 lb fresh mushrooms, sliced

Instructions

  1. For the marinade, mix wine, sliced onions, carrots, brandy, garlic, peppercorns, salt, parsley, and bay leaf in a large bowl
  2. Add beef and toss to evenly coat; cover the bowl with plastic wrap and marinate in the refrigerator for 2 days
  3. For the bourguignon, preheat oven to 150C
  4. Strain beef from the marinade and shake off excess; pat dry with paper towels
  5. Strain vegetables and set aside; reserve marinade
  6. Heat 2 tbsp of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat
  7. Add beef in batches; cook until browned on all sides
  8. Transfer browned beef into a separate medium bowl; set aside
  9. Add bacon to the same skillet; cook and stir until lightly browned
  10. Transfer bacon to the bowl with browned beef
  11. Drain skillet and return it to the heat
  12. Pour 1 cup of reserved marinade into the skillet; bring to a boil while scraping browned bits of food off the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon
  13. Return this liquid to the reserved marinade
  14. Heat remaining 2 tbsp oil in the same skillet
  15. Add chopped onions; cook and stir until just tender (~2-3 minutes)
  16. Stir in reserved onions and carrots from the marinade; mix well
  17. Use a slotted spoon to transfer vegetables into the bowl with beef
  18. Return the skillet to the heat; stir flour in pan drippings until browned (~1-2 minutes)
  19. Stir in garlic and tomato paste until combined
  20. Add beef broth, remaining reserved marinade, salt, and pepper
  21. Bring to a boil, whisking to remove any flour lumps; pour over beef mixture in the bowl
  22. Transfer beef mixture into a casserole or baking dish
  23. Cook in preheated oven until beef is tender (~3 hours)
  24. Stir occasionally and add water if needed
  25. About 10 minutes before serving, melt butter in a skillet over medium-high heat
  26. Add mushrooms and sauté until lightly browned (~5-7 minutes)
  27. Stir mushrooms into beef mixture until well combined
